pyLIMA.microlmagnification.amplification_USBL(separation, q, Xs, Ys, rho, tolerance=0.001)[source]¶

The uniform source binary lens amplification, based on the work of Valerio Bozza, thanks :) “Microlensing with an advanced contour integration algorithm: Green’s theorem to third order, error control,

optimal sampling and limb darkening ”,Bozza, Valerio 2010. Please cite the paper if you used this.

http://mnras.oxfordjournals.org/content/408/4/2188

Parameters:
  • s (float) – the projected normalised angular distance between the two bodies
  • q (float) – the mass ratio of the two bodies
  • Xs (array_like) – the horizontal positions of the source center in the source plane
  • Ys (array_like) – the vertical positions of the source center in the source plane
  • rho (float) – the normalised (to :math:`theta_E’) angular source star radius
  • tolerance (float) – the relative precision desired in the magnification
Returns:

the USBL magnification A_USBL(t) and the impact parameter U(t)
Return type:

array_like,array_like
